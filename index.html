Nippeli Information About Oulu

Oulu as a city sits firmly at the mouth of the Oulu River. It is the capital point of North Ostrobothnia and with its population of two hundred and seven thousand, it is the largest city in northern Finland.

Historically speaking the city was founded as a trade city by the king of Sweden Charles the Ninth in 1765. The name of the city is rooted in the Sámi word for flooding water. It grew due to its trade license bestowed by the founding king. It traded mostly in tar, wood and ships.

Its major modern specialties are culture and technology. It has historically produced many musicians and writers such as Teuvo Pakkala, Sara Wacklin and Kauko Röyhkä. Its cultural events such as Qstock and the world championships of air guitar playing are a good eye into the life in Oulu. It also hosts the “hockey team of northern Finland” in the Kärpät.

Technology wise it became the northern countries’ first host of a technology nexus in 1982 with the founding of Technopolis near the city’s university. As such Oulu is often called the Nordic Silicon Valley.

Other Information About Oulu

Welcome to Oulu! We think you might like your time here! Be you a student of the University or the Polytechnic it is good to be a student in this city. From simple and efficient bus routes to well supported biking routes, traversing the city on a budget is made extremely easy. Living too is made easy by lots of affordable living near the campuses or near major traffic ways. The student living organization PSOAS also aids in this regard. Eating either should not prove an issue since student lunches are arranged cheaply by the schools.
